The Pi Phenomenon

When a large block of mass M collides with a smaller block of mass m (which is against a wall), the total number of collisions approximates the digits of π.

Specifically, if M/m = 100d, the number of collisions equals the first d+1 digits of π.

Example: M/m = 100 (d=1) → 3 collisions (3.14)
